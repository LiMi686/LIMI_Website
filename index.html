i. About Me

Who Am I

I'm Li Mi (米力), a Data Science graduate student at the University of Arizona, actively transitioning into AI Engineering. My foundation is in data analysis and statistical modeling — and I'm now channeling that into building real AI systems: LLM pipelines, RAG architectures, and intelligent agents.

The turning point was Hack Arizona — a 36-hour hackathon where my team designed an AI system for community food access. We won the 🏆 Most Innovative Use of AI for the Public Good award from the Community Food Bank of Southern Arizona. That moment made it clear: I don't just want to analyze the world — I want to build systems that change it.

Outside of code, you'll find me under an autumn tree with a guitar — because both music and AI are about the same thing: finding the patterns that matter.

"The falling of a maple leaf and the training of a model — both are beautiful acts of letting go."
